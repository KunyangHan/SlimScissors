Abstract

Despite the great progress made by existing deep learning-based interactive segmentation algorithms where only a few clicks are sufficient to segment an object, these methods typically fail when segmenting objects with elongated thin structures (e.g., bicycle spokes), requiring extensive user inputs for correction. Recent effort reveals that the failure can be attributed to poor training data quality and class imbalance between thin and non-thin regions, and contributes a new synthetic dataset and a three-stream network design. Nevertheless, we observe that this work suffers from two limitations: 1) large performance gap when tested on real image domain; 2) extensive amounts of user interactions (clicks) are still needed to perform refinement if the thin structures are not well segmented. To address this, we develop Slim Scissors, which enables quick extraction of elongated thin parts by simply brushing some coarse scribbles. At the core of our approach is the novel idea of segmenting thin parts by learning to compare the original image to a synthetic background whose thin structures are absent. To this end, given coarsely drawn scribbles, we construct a synthetic background without the thin parts and train a thin object segmentation network based on such (image, background) pair. We show that such a design is model-agnostic and can be easily incorporated into existing state-of-the-art interactive segmentation network to enable an end-to-end trainable segmentation framework. To reduce the annotation burden, we further devise a similarity detection module, which enables the model to automatically synthesize background for other similar thin structures when given only one or two scribbles. We perform extensive experiments on COIFT, HRSOD, and ThinObject-5K. In particular, our Slim Scissors outperforms TOS-Net by 5.9% IoU_thin and 3.5% F score on the real dataset HRSOD, well demonstrating the superiority of our solution in extracting thin objects.
